Ben Thompson's Resumé

Research Experience

The Binary INformation on Open Clusters using SEDs (BINOCS) Project
Sep 2012 - Present
PhD Thesis Project

  • Developed a new method of detecting binary systems within star clusters that provides mass information on more stars, with better precision, and in far less time than currently-available methods.
  • Wrote analysis program to detect binary systems in datasets of tens of thousands of stars.
  • Parallelized Python analysis program through the use of OpenCL.

Learn more about the BINOCS project at its research page, or view its source code on GitHub.

The Sloan Digital Sky Survey IV Autoscheduler
Sep 2013 - Present

  • SDSS-IV is a continuation of the Sloan Digital Sky Survey, "one of the most ambitious and influential surveys in the history of astronomy," which consists of three science programs (APOGEE-II, eBOSS and MaNGA), and will run from Aug 2014 - 2020.
  • Wrote program which optimally chooses targets for observation by the SDSS-IV telescope for two of the three SDSS-IV science programs (APOGEE-II, eBOSS)
  • Linked programs with a PostgreSQL database to read in plate parameters for thousands of plates, and information on tens of thousands of past observations.
